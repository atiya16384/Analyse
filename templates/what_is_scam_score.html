What is a Scam Score?

Overview

The scam score is a numerical representation of how likely a piece of content (e.g., a message, email, or document) is to be fraudulent, deceptive, or malicious. A higher score indicates a higher likelihood of the content being a scam, while a lower score suggests the content is more likely legitimate.

How is the Scam Score Calculated?

The scam score is determined through a combination of:

  • Machine Learning: Our AI model analyzes text for patterns commonly found in scams, such as specific keywords, structures, or tones.
  • Heuristic Analysis: We evaluate elements like grammar, spelling, URLs, and domain structures for suspicious patterns.
  • Positive Indicators: Legitimate signals, such as proper grammar, trusted domains, and low keyword density, help reduce the score.

Each of these factors is assigned a weight to calculate the final score.

Why is the Scam Score Important?

Understanding the scam score helps you:

  • Identify Risks: Quickly determine whether a piece of content is likely to be fraudulent.
  • Make Informed Decisions: Avoid falling victim to phishing attacks or scams.
  • Protect Sensitive Information: Safeguard your personal and financial details from potential threats.
Scam Score Levels

The scam score is categorized into three levels to help you assess the risk:

  • Low Risk (0% - 35%): The content is likely safe, but vigilance is always recommended.
  • Medium Risk (36% - 70%): Exercise caution. There may be some indicators of potential risk.
  • High Risk (71% - 100%): The content is highly likely to be fraudulent. Avoid interacting with it and consider reporting it.
